After deciding that the final focus of my project would be pet separation anxiety, the next step was defining how this community should actually function. I believe the most important part of this platform should not simply be posting or browsing content, but helping users match with suitable solutions and gain more practical experience and knowledge. This gradually became the core feature of the project, which I call the “Pet Situation Matching System.

The most important part of this system is the creation of a pet profile. When users first join the community, the platform will guide them to build a profile for their pet. This profile may include information such as the pet type, age, temperament, common behaviours, and how long the pet is usually left home alone. This would make the overall experience more personalised and also allow the platform to continuously recommend more relevant cases over time. I also considered that pets’ behaviours and daily routines may change over time, so the website would regularly remind users to update their profiles. This helps the recommendations stay up to date and allows the system to match based on the pet’s current condition rather than relying on outdated information.

The second important part is the matching process itself. Users can search for support based on more detailed situations instead of simply browsing broad categories. For example, a user may want to find cases involving a young, high-energy dog that scratches the door when left alone for more than six hours. Instead of forcing users to slowly search through large amounts of scattered posts, I want the platform to automatically help them match with the most relevant community experiences and care routines.

I also started considering how AI could improve the overall user experience. I want pet owners to feel as though they have a supportive assistant or “care manager,” rather than simply browsing posts. The AI would summarise patterns across multiple community cases and provide several of the most common and effective solutions. For example, the system may discover that many owners of anxious dogs achieved positive results after combining puzzle toys, scent-based comfort items, and exercise before leaving the house. At the same time, the platform would also recommend highly liked, frequently saved, or highly rated community posts.

However, I still want this platform to prioritise real user experiences rather than relying entirely on automated recommendations. The role of AI would be to organise and summarise information, rather than replace veterinarians or provide direct medical advice. Because of this, the original community cases shared by users would still remain fully visible underneath the AI-generated summaries, allowing users to continue reading detailed experiences shared by real pet owners.
