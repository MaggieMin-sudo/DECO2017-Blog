When I first started thinking about my Bla+Bla community hub, my initial idea was to create a pet community. This topic felt personal and meaningful to me because I have a lovely dog at home, and I am genuinely interested in pets. Since I already have some experience as a pet owner, I felt that I could understand this user group more easily. Pet owners often want to learn more about animal care, behaviour, and daily pet routines, so they may be willing to explore different platforms to find useful information. Also, people’s love for their pets is strongly emotional. Many pet owners are willing to share stories, experiences, advice, and everyday moments with their animals.

However, after thinking more carefully about this idea, I realised that a general pet community might not be strong enough for this project. If the platform only allows users to post daily updates, share cute photos, or exchange basic advice, then it would not be very different from existing platforms such as Reddit or Facebook. These platforms already allow pet owners to share experiences and communicate with other people. Therefore, if I want my Bla+Bla community hub to attract users, it needs to offer something more specific and more useful than a normal social media space. This made me start narrowing down my concept and thinking about what kind of pet-related problem the community should focus on.

Therefore, I started to look for problems that many pet owners may commonly experience, especially problems related to pet behaviour. One issue that appears frequently is separation anxiety. It is very common for pets to stay at home alone, because owners often need to go to work, attend university, or manage other daily responsibilities. They cannot stay with their pets all day.

This situation can create many worries for pet owners. They may be concerned that their pets will bark, destroy furniture, become emotionally distressed, or repeatedly scratch at the door because they want their owner to return. In some cases, this behaviour could even cause the pet to hurt itself. Although existing platforms may provide some related advice, the information is often scattered and difficult to search through. Owners may struggle to quickly find cases that are similar to the specific problem they are facing.

Because of this, I believe my Bla+Bla community hub can focus on helping pet owners filter relevant cases and share care experiences in a more structured way. Instead of becoming a general pet discussion space, the platform can help users find advice, routines, and examples that better match their own pet’s situation.
