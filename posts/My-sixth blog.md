In this final blog post, I feel that I have almost completely confirmed what the platform should eventually become. At the beginning of the project, my ideas were still very broad. I originally imagined it as a general pet community with many social features and different kinds of interaction. However, over time, I gradually narrowed the concept down into a platform focused on solving a specific problem.

The biggest change was the project gradually focusing on separation anxiety and situation-based matching. In the early stages of the project, I spent a lot of time thinking about social interaction, discussion spaces, and community features. However, I later realised that the most valuable part of the platform was not the social aspect itself, but its ability to help users quickly find what they need, reduce their anxiety, and help pets receive better care.

I no longer want the platform to feel like a fast-moving social media space filled with endless interactions and content. Instead, I want the platform to feel more focused and supportive. The goal of the platform is not to maximise interaction between users, but to help users find reassurance and useful solutions.

At this stage, the platform mainly revolves around several connected systems, including pet profiles, the matching system, care case sharing, saved content, and AI-supported summaries. The platform no longer treats community experiences as random discussion posts. Instead, it treats them more like a large reusable knowledge library that can help future users facing similar situations.

The role of AI within the system has also become much clearer. AI is mainly responsible for organising and summarising community knowledge, while real user experiences still remain the core of the platform. I still believe that AI cannot fully express the love pet owners have for their pets, and it cannot fully provide the warmth and trust created through real human experiences.

I also learned that the project needed to become simpler. Adding more features would not necessarily make the platform more valuable. In many cases, reducing unnecessary complexity actually makes the system clearer, more purposeful, and easier to use. Removing the comment section became one of my favourite design decisions in the project.

From this point onward, I want to focus more on turning these ideas into working prototypes, interface designs, interaction flows, and technical implementation.
