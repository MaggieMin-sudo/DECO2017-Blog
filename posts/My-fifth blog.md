The next step was thinking more carefully about scope and accessibility. I briefly mentioned this in my previous blog post. At first, I considered many different features, including real-time chat, large discussion sections, more complex user interactions, and even more advanced AI systems. However, I later realised that these features would not only make the platform more difficult to use, but would also make the development process much more complicated.

Because of this, I decided to keep only the features that directly support the core goal of the platform. The most important purpose of this system is to help users find useful care experiences for pets with separation anxiety. Features that are not strongly connected to this goal are no longer being considered. As I mentioned in the previous blog post, I decided not to include a real-time chat system or large comment sections. I believe these features would not only increase system complexity, but could also create more noise and negative interactions within the community, which may cause already anxious users to feel even more stressed.

This decision is also closely connected to accessibility and usability. I want the platform to remain clear and easy to navigate, especially for users who may already feel anxious or emotionally overwhelmed. Because of this, I started thinking more carefully about how to reduce visual clutter. I do not want users to see large amounts of information on a single page. Instead, I want the interface to guide users through the content step by step.

I also considered that many users may browse the platform on their phones while commuting, during work breaks, or while checking on their pets throughout the day. Because of this, the interface needs to remain clear and easy to use across both desktop and mobile devices. I want the entire matching process to feel very simple and accessible on different screen sizes.

Since the platform relies heavily on filtering and structured information, users need to clearly understand the navigation system and different interaction states. I want to use consistent layouts, clear colour contrast, and obvious labels so different users can use the platform more comfortably and easily.

Another important decision was limiting the role of AI within the platform. Earlier in the project, I imagined a more advanced and complex AI recommendation system. However, I later realised that overly complicated AI systems would be difficult to explain, could reduce user trust, and would also be unrealistic to fully implement within the project timeline. Because of this, I finally decided that the main role of AI should be organising and summarising community knowledge rather than completely replacing real user experiences.

A platform does not become better simply because it has more features. In many situations, simplifying the overall experience and focusing on the most meaningful interactions can create a platform that is easier to use and more supportive for users.
