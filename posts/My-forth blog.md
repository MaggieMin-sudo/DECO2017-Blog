The next step was thinking more carefully about the emotional experience of the platform. At first, I mainly focused on the matching system and the information itself. However, I later realised that many users may already feel anxious, worried, or even guilty about leaving their pets home alone when they come to the platform. Because of this, I do not want the platform to feel cold or overly technical.

I no longer want to design it like a fast-scrolling social media feed. Instead, I want the community to feel calmer, warmer, and easier to navigate. I want users to focus on finding genuinely helpful information rather than becoming overwhelmed by large amounts of content. Because of this, I started considering cleaner layouts, softer visual hierarchy, and more controlled amounts of information on each page.

I also started thinking about how trust should be built within the platform. Since this community discusses pet behaviour and emotional care, users need to feel that the information is both real and helpful. Because of this, AI should not completely replace real user experiences. The role of AI should be to organise and summarise information while still preserving the original community cases so users can continue reading real experiences shared by other pet owners. I believe this balance is very important because users are more likely to trust real experiences from other pet owners than fully automated recommendations.

Another important decision was reducing unnecessary social features. Many existing social platforms constantly encourage users to interact with each other, but I do not think this experience fits the purpose of this project. Users are not visiting the platform simply for entertainment. Most users are actually looking for reassurance, advice, or solutions that fit their pet’s situation. Because of this, I want the overall experience to feel more focused and supportive rather than highly stimulating. For this reason, I am considering including only simple features such as likes and saved posts, without adding comments. I do not want aggressive or negative language to create more anxiety for pet owners.

I also hope the platform can reduce the feeling that “I am the only person experiencing this problem.” Many pet owners may believe that only their pets experience behaviours such as barking, pacing, or separation anxiety. By showing similar cases and shared care routines from other users, the platform can help users understand that these problems are actually very common while also creating a stronger sense of emotional support.

This platform is not only a matching system or an information tool. It is also a space that provides emotional support for pet owners. Because of this, the interface design, content structure, and recommendation system all need to be designed around clarity, trust, and emotional comfort rather than focusing only on efficiency.
